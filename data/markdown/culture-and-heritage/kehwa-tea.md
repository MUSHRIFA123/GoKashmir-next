**Kashmiri Kehwa Tea**, also known as Khawa, is a traditional green tea infused with saffron, almonds, cardamom, and occasionally cinnamon. ## ‚ú® Features of Kashmiri Kehwa

### **Traditional Green Tea Base**  
![Kashmiri Green Tea](/img/attractions/kashmiri-green-tea.jpg)  
The base of Kehwa is high-quality green tea leaves, which provide a refreshing, slightly bitter taste that balances the sweetness of added spices. Green tea is known for its health benefits, including antioxidants and digestion support.  

Infusing green tea with saffron threads, cardamom, and sometimes cinnamon transforms it into a flavorful, aromatic beverage, perfect for warming up in the chilly Kashmiri winters or complementing traditional sweets.  

---

### **Saffron and Almond Infusion**  
![Saffron Almond Kehwa](/img/attractions/saffron-almond-kehwa.jpg)  
Saffron, the golden spice grown in Kashmir, adds color, fragrance, and a delicate taste to Kehwa. Almonds provide a nutty flavor and texture, enhancing both nutrition and aroma. This combination gives Kehwa its signature appearance and makes it a ceremonial drink during festivals.  

Saffron-infused Kehwa is considered luxurious and is often served to guests as a mark of hospitality, reflecting Kashmir‚Äôs tradition of welcoming visitors with warmth and flavor.

![Kashmiri Kehwa Variants](/img/attractions/kehwa-variants.jpg)  
While traditional Kehwa includes saffron and almonds, variations may feature rose petals, cinnamon, or cardamom powder, depending on the season and occasion. During festivals like Navroz, Eid, or weddings, special blends of Kehwa are prepared with extra care.

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Kehwa?  
Kehwa, also called Khawa, is a traditional Kashmiri green tea brewed with saffron, almonds, cardamom, and sometimes cinnamon, often served warm during winters or festivals.  

### Where can I try authentic Kehwa?  
Traditional houseboats, cafes in Srinagar, local markets, and craft villages across the Kashmir Valley serve fresh, aromatic Kehwa.  

### Is Kehwa sweet or savory?  
Kehwa has a delicate, mildly sweet flavor from saffron and almonds, balanced with green tea bitterness and sometimes cardamom or cinnamon.  

### Can I buy ingredients to make Kehwa at home?  
Yes, local markets sell high-quality Kashmiri saffron, almonds, and green tea leaves for authentic home preparation.  

### Are there different types of Kehwa?  
Yes, traditional saffron-almond Kehwa, rose-infused variants, cinnamon or cardamom blends, and festival-specific special blends exist across the valley.  

### Is Kehwa served during festivals?  
Absolutely. Kehwa is a staple during Navroz, Eid, weddings, and cultural celebrations, symbolizing hospitality and tradition in Kashmir.
