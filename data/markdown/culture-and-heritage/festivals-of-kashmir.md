## ‚ú® Popular Festivals of Kashmir

**Navroz (Kashmiri New Year)**  
![Navroz Festival](/img/attractions/navroz-festival.jpg)  
Celebrated in March, Navroz marks the start of the Kashmiri Hindu and Shia Muslim new year. The festival is vibrant with traditional music, cultural performances, and special dishes prepared for families and friends.  

Streets and homes are decorated, and people exchange greetings, sweets, and gifts. Navroz is a joyous occasion highlighting Kashmir‚Äôs rich heritage, unity, and festive spirit, offering tourists a chance to experience authentic local traditions and community celebrations.  

**Kashmir Autumn Festival**  
![Kashmir Autumn Festival](/img/attractions/kashmir-autumn-festival.jpg)  
Held in October, the Kashmir Autumn Festival celebrates the harvest season with folk music, traditional dances, handicrafts, and seasonal foods. The valley comes alive with cultural performances and colorful fairs.  

The autumn scenery adds a magical touch to the festivities. Visitors can explore local traditions, taste regional delicacies, and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere, making it a perfect opportunity to experience Kashmir‚Äôs rich culture and natural beauty during the fall season.  

**Shab-e-Meraj and Eid Celebrations**  
![Shab-e-Meraj Eid](/img/attractions/shab-e-meraj-eid.jpg)  
These significant Islamic festivals are celebrated with prayers, communal feasts, and vibrant cultural activities. Streets and markets are beautifully decorated, creating a festive ambiance throughout Kashmir.  

Families gather for meals, exchange greetings, and participate in local rituals. Tourists can witness the spiritual and social unity of Kashmiris, explore lively markets, and enjoy traditional sweets, gaining insight into the region‚Äôs religious and cultural heritage.  

**Tulip Festival**  
![Tulip Festival](/img/attractions/tulip-festival.jpg)  
Held in spring at the Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden, the Tulip Festival showcases millions of colorful tulips. Visitors can enjoy live music, cultural performances, and local handicrafts amidst the scenic gardens.  

Photographers and nature lovers flock to capture the mesmerizing views. The festival blends Kashmir‚Äôs natural beauty with vibrant cultural festivities, offering a peaceful, colorful, and immersive experience for travelers in the heart of the valley.  

**Baisakhi and Harvest Fairs**  
![Baisakhi Festival](/img/attractions/baisakhi-festival.jpg)  
Celebrated in April, Baisakhi marks the harvest season with lively music, traditional dances, and colorful fairs. Local produce and handicrafts are showcased for visitors to enjoy.  

Tourists can interact with local communities, sample seasonal Kashmiri delicacies, and experience the joy and traditions of rural life. Baisakhi provides an authentic cultural experience while highlighting the agrarian lifestyle of Kashmir.  

**Heritage Festivals in Pahalgam and Gulmarg**  
![Heritage Festivals Pahalgam Gulmarg](/img/attractions/heritage-festivals.jpg)  
These festivals highlight Kashmir‚Äôs rich heritage through traditional sports, horse races, folk music, and dance performances. Held in scenic locations, they showcase the valley‚Äôs historical traditions and artistry.  

Visitors can enjoy performances by local artists, explore craft stalls, and witness authentic rituals. The festivals offer an engaging blend of entertainment, cultural immersion, and picturesque landscapes, making them a must-visit for travelers seeking Kashmir‚Äôs heritage firsthand.
