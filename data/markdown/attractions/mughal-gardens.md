The **Mughal Gardens of Srinagar**, especially **Shalimar Bagh** and **Nishat Bagh**, are magnificent examples of Persian-style landscape architecture introduced during the Mughal era. Designed with symmetrical layouts, terraced lawns, and flowing fountains, these gardens reflect the Mughal philosophy of harmony between nature, water, and architecture.

Set against the scenic **Zabarwan Hills** and overlooking **Dal Lake**, the gardens offer breathtaking views throughout the year. Spring and summer bring vibrant flower blooms, while autumn transforms the gardens with warm golden hues. Together, Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh stand as cultural, historical, and architectural highlights of Kashmir, attracting visitors, photographers, and history enthusiasts alike.

## üöï Travel Information

Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh are located along the **foothills of the Zabarwan Range** in Srinagar and are easily accessible by **taxi or private vehicle** from any part of the city. The gardens are situated close to Dal Lake, making it convenient to combine the visit with nearby attractions.

The **best time to visit is during spring and summer**, when the gardens are in full bloom. Autumn is also a beautiful season to visit, offering golden foliage and fewer crowds. Morning and late afternoon visits provide pleasant weather and excellent lighting for photography.
